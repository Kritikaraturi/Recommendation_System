📖 What is UI/UX Design?

UI/UX Design is the process of creating digital products that are useful, easy to use, and delightful to interact with. UX (User Experience) design focuses on the overall feel of the product and how users navigate through it. It involves user research, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing. UI (User Interface) design focuses on the visual elements — colors, typography, icons, buttons, and spacing — that users see and interact with. A Creative Technologist bridges the gap between design and technology, combining creative thinking with technical skills to build innovative digital experiences. Together, these roles are essential for creating websites, mobile apps, software, and other digital products that people love to use.

🛣️ Complete Learning Roadmap

1. Learn Design Fundamentals
Understand color theory, typography, layout, spacing, visual hierarchy, and design principles.
2. Master Figma
Figma is the industry standard tool for UI/UX design. Learn to create wireframes, prototypes, and design systems.
3. User Research & Psychology
Learn how to conduct user interviews, surveys, usability testing, and understand user psychology.
4. Wireframing & Prototyping
Create low-fidelity wireframes and high-fidelity interactive prototypes to test your designs.
5. Design Systems & UI Kits
Build reusable components and maintain consistency across large projects using design systems.
6. Build Portfolio
Showcase your best projects, case studies, and design process to attract employers.
7. Interview Preparation & Job Applications
Prepare for design interviews, whiteboard challenges, and portfolio presentations.
